Three Bears Motel is an ideal base for exploring the wild beauty of Lincoln, Montana. Conveniently located on Highway 200, our motel provides easy access to the area’s top outdoor destinations.

Nested near the Blackfoot River and the Bob Marshall Wilderness, the area offers year-round recreation including fishing, hiking, snowmobiling, and access to the Continental Divide Trail (CDT).

Wildlife is part of everyday life here. Visitors often spot elk, deer, bald eagles, river otters, and other native species. With its pristine forests, clear rivers, and mountain landscapes, Lincoln delivers an authentic Montana outdoor experience in every season.

Local Life, History & Events

Lincoln, Montana began in the late 1800s as a supply hub for miners and prospectors. Today, the town blends its frontier roots with a welcoming small-town spirit and a strong connection to the outdoors.

Visitors can experience local culture year-round at Blackfoot Pathways: Sculpture in the Wild, a free, wheelchair-accessible outdoor art park that showcases large-scale works by regional artists.

Throughout the year, Lincoln comes alive with community events, including:

  • Race to the Sky international dogsled race
  • Rockin’ the Rockies summer music festival
  • Fourth of July celebrations and fireworks
  • Seasonal farmers markets and local artisan pop-ups

Whether you're visiting for an event or simply enjoying the slower pace of small-town Montana, you'll find a friendly community and plenty to explore during your stay.
