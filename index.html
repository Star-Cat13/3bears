Embrace Local History and Culture

The town of Lincoln was established in the late 1800s as a supply hub for miners and prospectors. Its history is deeply intertwined with the stories of pioneers, adventurers, and the challenges they faced in the pursuit of wealth and opportunity. Over the years, Lincoln transitioned from a mining center to a more diverse community, embracing its natural beauty and becoming a destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

A Haven for Art Enthusiasts

Indulge your artistic side and explore Lincoln's Blackfoot Pathways Sculpture in the Wild. It's open year-round, wheelchair accessible, and free to visit!

Throughout the vibrant summer months, Lincoln comes alive with captivating energy, hosting delightful pop-up markets that showcase the incredible talents of local artists hailing from all corners of Montana.

Lincoln, Montana's Enchanting Nature and Wildlife

Nestled in the heart of the rugged Rocky Mountains, Lincoln, Montana, boasts a breathtaking natural wonderland that beckons to wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. With its pristine forests, crystal-clear rivers, and majestic peaks, the region offers a sanctuary for an array of fascinating wildlife.

Visitors to Lincoln can witness the awe-inspiring sight of graceful elk meandering through lush meadows, while bald eagles soar majestically overhead. The area is also home to elusive black bears, playful river otters, and an assortment of native bird species, creating an enchanting tapestry of biodiversity. Whether exploring the Bob Marshall Wilderness Complex or admiring the serenity of the Blackfoot River, the harmonious coexistence of humans and nature in Lincoln, Montana, promises an unforgettable and immersive tourist attraction, celebrating the untamed beauty of Montana.
